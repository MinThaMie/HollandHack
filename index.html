Gender Equality

"A gender-equal society would be one where the word 'gender' does not exist: where everyone can be themselves."

Gloria Steinem

The topic 'Gender Equality' is nowadays more a topic of gender inequality. All over the world and unfortuantly also in Europe women have a weaker position in the labor market. The United Nation Development Program created an inequality index and ranked almost every country on the world. The Netherlands are ranked seventh and Germany is ranked third. This page will give you an overview of gender inequality in both Germany and the Netherlands. Based on different data sources especially EuroStat, since those data can be easily combined and compared. The sole purpose is to raise awareness to improve this iniquity.

Germany

7.5

Netherlands

7.7

This score is an index based on three categories shown on this website below. Jobs, education and wages are all considered in creating a relevant index.

Jobs

Summary

In general both countries are really close together on labor paricipation of women, but as shown in the graph where this is devide by sectors there are some mayor differences. In the Netherlands there are more women working knowledge-intensive services, where Germany has more women in, for example, less knowledge intensive market services, whole sale and retail trade and manifacturing. More women are working full time in Germany, in the Netherlands this is the other way around.

Germany: 8.5
Netherlands: 7.8

Education

Summary

Education statistics show high participation numbers for females in both Germany and The Netherlands. Germany has more people with at least some secondary education, The Netherlands has more females in higher education (ISCED 5 or higher). This means that The Netherlands might have relatively higher educated women; they also have more women without secondary education.

Germany: 7.9
Netherlands: 8.2

Wages

Summary

The graph above shows the gender payment gap and strongly indicates that women in the netherlands are payed more like the men in the same position.Luckely the graphs shows a trend nearing an equal wage for men and women.

Germany: 6.2
Netherlands: 7.3
